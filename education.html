Earthquake Education & Terminology

We have compiled explanations of frequently used basic terms so you can interpret earthquake data better.

ğŸ“Š Magnitude vs Intensity

Magnitude: It is the mathematical measure of the energy released at the earthquake's source. It is instrumental and does not change (e.g., 7.4).

Intensity: It is the effect of the earthquake on the earth's surface. It is determined by the damage caused to buildings, nature, and people, and varies from region to region.

ğŸŒ Focal Depth

It indicates how many kilometers below the ground the earthquake occurred. Shallow earthquakes (0-60 km) are felt in a smaller area but can be more destructive. Deep earthquakes are felt over a much wider area but are generally less destructive.

âš¡ Fault Line

These are lines where rocks in the earth's crust break and shift. Earthquakes usually occur due to the sudden release of energy accumulated along these lines.

ğŸ“‰ Aftershock

Smaller tremors that occur in the same area after the main earthquake. They can last for weeks or even months until the earth's crust finds its balance.
